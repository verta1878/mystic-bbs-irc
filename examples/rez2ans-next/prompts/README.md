# Source Artwork Prompts

These prompt guides help you create or commission source artwork that survives ANSI conversion. They work with most image-generation tools, but they are equally useful as a brief for a human artist.

The important idea is simple: make the subject readable at a small size before converting it. Strong shapes, limited colors, clean outlines, and a quiet background produce better ANSI than fine texture or photorealistic detail.

## Use a prompt

1. Choose a guide below.
2. Replace the bracketed words with your idea.
3. Request a clean, high-resolution image with no embedded lettering.
4. Crop and resize in Rez2ANS Next before conversion.
5. Add final lettering in an ANSI editor rather than relying on generated text.

## General negative prompt

Add this where your image tool supports negative prompts:

`photorealistic detail, tiny text, words, watermark, signature, cluttered background, thin lines, soft focus, noise, busy texture, lens flare, excessive gradients, small objects`

Always use artwork you are allowed to convert and distribute.
